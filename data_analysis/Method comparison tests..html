I have solution values from two different methods on several different problems, and the solution values are not normally distributed. What is the best statistical test for comparing one method to another?
1 / 1

If the solution values are not normally distributed, you may consider using a non-parametric test to compare the two methods. One common non-parametric test for comparing two independent samples is the Wilcoxon rank-sum test, also known as the Mann-Whitney U test.

The Wilcoxon rank-sum test is used to test the null hypothesis that two independent samples come from the same distribution. It does not require any assumptions about the shape of the distribution or the equality of variances between the two groups. Instead, it compares the ranks of the values in the two samples.

You can perform the Wilcoxon rank-sum test in most statistical software packages such as R, Python, or Excel. The test returns a p-value, which represents the probability of observing a difference between the two methods as extreme as the one observed, assuming the null hypothesis is true. If the p-value is less than your chosen significance level, typically 0.05, you can reject the null hypothesis and conclude that the two methods have different solution values.

How do I do the wilcoxon rank sum test in python?
1 / 1

To perform the Wilcoxon rank-sum test in Python, you can use the scipy.stats module. Here is an example of how to use the scipy.stats.ranksums function:

python
from scipy.stats import ranksums

# two sets of solution values
method1 = [1.5, 2.3, 3.7, 4.1, 5.9]
method2 = [1.2, 2.7, 3.2, 4.6, 5.1]

# perform Wilcoxon rank-sum test
statistic, pvalue = ranksums(method1, method2)

# print results
print("Wilcoxon rank-sum test")
print(f"Statistic: {statistic:.3f}")
print(f"P-value: {pvalue:.3f}")

In this example, method1 and method2 are the two sets of solution values that you want to compare. The ranksums function returns the test statistic and p-value.

If the p-value is less than your chosen significance level (e.g., 0.05), you can reject the null hypothesis and conclude that the two methods have different solution values. If the p-value is greater than your significance level, you cannot reject the null hypothesis and conclude that the two methods do not have significantly different solution values.

what does it mean if my p-value is 1 from the manwhitney u test?
1 / 1

If the p-value from the Mann-Whitney U test is 1, it means that the observed difference between the two methods' solution values is not statistically significant at any conventional level of significance (e.g., Î± = 0.05).

In hypothesis testing, the p-value represents the probability of obtaining a test statistic as extreme as the one observed, assuming the null hypothesis is true. A p-value of 1 indicates that the observed difference between the two methods is entirely consistent with the null hypothesis, which states that the two samples come from the same distribution.

Therefore, you would fail to reject the null hypothesis and conclude that there is not enough evidence to suggest that the two methods have different solution values. The lack of statistical significance suggests that any observed differences in the solution values between the two methods could be due to random chance or sampling variability.
